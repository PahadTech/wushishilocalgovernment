HISTORICAL LITERATURE OF WUSHISHI LOCAL GOVERNMENT AREA, NIGER STATE

The Wushishi local government is part of what remained of the defunct Wushishi local government after the creation of more states and local government during the regime of Late General Sani Abacha, on October 1, 1996. This exercise was carried Arthur Mbanefo’s Committee on States and Local Government creation in the country. During the exercise, the present Mashegu Local Government, was carved out from Wushishi local government, to form one of the six newly create local government in the state in addition to the nineteen existing ones. This increased the number of local government in the state to its present twenty five. before that balkanization, the defunct Wushishi local government itself was create on august 27, 1991, during the regime of General Ibrahim BadamasiBabangida from the yet defunct Mariga Local government with headquarters at Kontagora.

MINERAL RESOURCES

It has often been said that Niger State is practically sitting on gold. Wushishi local government. Like most local government in the state, has a large quantity of gold deposits. The lower valley of the State Polytechnic at Zungeru, is especially reported to contain large, commercial deposits of gold in its bowels Since half of the local government is surrounded by water, fish is another aquatic resource that abound in the area. The area has made quite a name for itself in this regard since time immemorial.

CLIMATE CONDITION

Temperatures rarely drop below or rise above 102°F in the humid, partly cloudy dry season of this hot and oppressive climate, which ranges from 61°F to 96°F.
From February 8 to April 22, the hot season, with an average daily high temperature exceeding 94°F, lasts for 2.4 months. Wushishi experiences the warmest month of the year in April, with an average high of 94°F and low of 76°F.
With an average daily maximum temperature below 87°F, the chilly season lasts for 3.4 months, from June 25 to October 4. With an average low of 62°F and high of 90°F, December is the coldest month of the year in Wushishi.
The average proportion of sky that is covered by clouds in Wushishi varies significantly seasonally throughout the year.
In Wushishi, the clearer season starts about November 4 and lasts for 3.7 months, coming to a close around February 24.
In Wushishi, January is the clearest month of the year, with the sky remaining clear, mostly clear, or partly overcast 57% of the time.
Beginning about February 24 and lasting for 8.3 months, the cloudier season ends around November 4.
May is the cloudiest month of the year in Wushishi, with the sky being overcast or mostly cloudy 82% of the time on average during this month.

TOURSIM

Arguably, the most notable place for tourism in the local government is Zungeru, the second most populous town in the area. It played a significant role in shapping the political history of Nigeria.
It was at zungeru that the amalgamation of both the Northern and the Southern protectorate took place in Febuary 1914, by Lord Frederick Lugard. Largely because of this, the town become the first capital of Nigeria, a status she enjoyed for seven months (February 1914, - August 1914). But even before this development, it was the capital of northern Nigeria, and had occupied this position from 1902-1916, when the capital was finally moved to Kaduna by the colonial masters. Relics of the structures put in place by the British are somewhat preserved by the state government and is now one of the main tourist attractions in the state.
Another interesting thing to know about Zungeru is that was in the town that the British Royal Niger Constabulary Force were merged with officers from other units to form what was then known as the West Africa Frontier force ( W.A.F.F), in 1897. The conquest of Northern Nigeria was made possible through the use of this force by the British. The plans for the capture of prominent Northern towns such as Bauch, Kano and Sokoto, were conceived and launched in Zungeru. The W.A.F.F. Barracks, as well as other military installations of the forces, are still being preserved by the state government as yet another tourist point in the state.
The town is also the birth-place of some of Nigeria’s most notable personalities.The first indigenous Governor –General, later first president of the federal Republic of Nigeria, the Owelle of Onitsha, Late Dr. NamdiAzikiwe, was born in Zungeru.
A village called 30km away from Wushishi, the local government Headquarters, served as a port for the British Royal Niger Company (R.N.C.). commercial goods and other materials used in the crection of the new Capital at Zungeru, were transported through rails from the village to the town. Remnants of the R.N.C. structure can still be found in the village. The tomb of a famous nomadic Islamic Scholar, ShehuAbdulraman, is at Cheji, 35 km away from Wushishi. He lived in the area. Propagating Islam during the time of the Fulani Jihadists. His tomb in the village is visited by Muslim faithful from far and near, and a mosque was built by the present local government council by the side of his tomb. His foot-print is reported to have appeared on a rock at Gwasakun, 50 Km away from Wushishi, and is preserved by the natives.
